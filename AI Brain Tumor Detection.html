// Detection Scope

Detectable Tumor Types

NeuraScan is trained to classify three primary types of brain tumors, plus cases with no detectable tumor.

Most Common

Glioma

Arising from glial cells, gliomas are the most frequent malignant brain tumors. They include glioblastoma, astrocytoma, and oligodendroglioma subtypes. NeuraScan achieves 98.2% sensitivity for glioma detection.

Benign / Malignant

Meningioma

Originating from the meninges, these tumors often grow slowly. They are the most common primary brain tumor overall and are usually benign but may require surgery depending on size and location.

Endocrine

Pituitary Tumor

Pituitary adenomas grow in the pituitary gland and may affect hormone regulation. Early detection via MRI is key to successful treatment, often through minimally invasive surgery.
